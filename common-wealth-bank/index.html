Tips for Financial Cybersecurity

Watch for Unusual Behavior in Your Account

Regularly monitor your financial accounts for any unusual behavior, so you can identify problems right away. For example, keep an eye out for unfamiliar or unauthorized transactions, missing funds, or unauthorized updates to your account information. If you notice any issues with your account, contact your bank immediately.

Keep Your Software Up to Date

The software on your devices should be kept up to date to protect against security vulnerabilities. You should update your operating system, security software, and other frequently used programs regularly. Where possible, you should set them to update automatically.

Practice Good Password Hygiene

Create strong passwords that use a mixture of letters, numbers, and special characters. Do not reuse passwords between accounts, and keep them in a secure place.

Use Multifactor Authentication

Use multifactor authentication on your financial accounts. Multifactor authentication requires both a password and a secondary authentication method, such as a text sent to you via text message or email. This secondary form of authentication makes it much more difficult for a bad actor to take over your account.

Avoid Clicking on Unknown Links

To protect yourself from malicious links, you should avoid clicking on links that you do not recognize. To avoid the risk of falling for a phishing scam, you should always enter the URL for your financial website directly into your web browser, rather than clicking on an external link.

Only Share Personal Information Over Encrypted Websites

When shopping online or otherwise sharing your banking information, only share your data through encrypted sites. You can recognize these sites by checking that their web address begin with "https." Sites that begin with "http" are not secure.

Don't Share Personal Information Over Public Wi-Fi Networks

Information shared over public Wi-Fi can be vulnerable to attacks because these networks typically use out-dated encryption. Additionally, hackers can create public networks that directly intercept information that you share. You should only share financial information over a trusted, encrypted network.

Avoid Accessing Private Accounts On Public Devices

Avoid using public devices to access your financial accounts, as they may contain software that records sensitive information like passwords or other login information. If you need to use a public device to access your accounts, make sure to log out after you've finished. Then, delete your Temporary Internet Files, Cache data, and History from your web browser.
